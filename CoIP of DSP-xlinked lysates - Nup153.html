Nup153 coIP from DSP-xlinked nuc lysates

Rae Cho / July 17, 2024
⚠️ This is a tentative version.



Workflow

  1. Protein A-bead preparation (Day 1: ~1 hr)
  2. Pre-clearing & CoIP (Day 2: ~4 hours)
  3. Washes & Elution (Day 2: ~3 hours)
  4. Western blotting (Day 3-4)

Key materials

  • Protein A magnetic beads (Thermo 10001D)
    • Protein A is for rabbit IgGs. For mouse IgGs, use Protein G bead.
    • Parafilm the bottle after use.
  • Anti-POI from rabbit (Nup153 in example below; preferrably polyclonal)
  • Neg IgG
    • CST 2729
    • Proteintech 30000-0-AP
  • PBS + 1% BSA + 0.01% Tween-20 (abbreviated “PBS-B/T”)
    • 40 ml PBS
    • 0.4 gram BSA
    • 40 ul x 10% Tween-20
  • RIPA buffer, 1 M DTT, and 0.5 M EDTA
  • Magnetic separation stand
  • DSP-crosslinked nuclear lysates
    • ‼️ Since the lysate is prepared in 6 M urea, it has to be dilute 7-fold in TBS or similar (e.g., 250 ul lysate + 1500 ul TBS) so that the final urea concentration is below 1 M. See Yang et al., ACS Omega, 2024 for IgG-urea-temperature denaturation curve. Staahl, J Neuro, 2013 did coIP with increasing concentration of urea (Figure 3; 0, 1, 2.5, and 5 M).

  2. (For all six tubes) Using the magnet, remove sup and wash with 1 ml PBS-B/T x 3 times (1, 3, and 5 min). Rotate “B” tubes at 4’C overnight.
  3. Dilute antibodies as below and add the solutions to “A” tubes. Rotate the mixtures at 4’ overnight.
    1. 6 ug neg IgG in 300 ul PBS-B/T (will be used for pre-clearing) label “3-1” below A
    2. 6 ug neg IgG in 300 ul PBS-B/T (will be used for pre-clearing) label “3-2” below A
    3. 6 ug neg IgG in 300 ul PBS-B/T (will be used for coIP) label “3-3” below A
    4. 6 ug anti-Nup153 in 300 ul PBS-B/T (will be used for coIP) label “3-4” below A


2. Pre-clearing & CoIP

  1. Wash antibody-bound beads 3-1 and 3-2 above using 1 ml of PBS-B/T x 3 time (1, 3, and 5 min).
  2. Meanwhile, thaw and save 40 ul DSP-crosslinked lysates. This is input. Store at -20’C.
  3. 1st pre-clearing (bead + neg IgG): Add lysates to 3-1 and 3-2, and rotate at 4’C for 60 min.
  4. 2nd pre-clearing (bead-only): Remove sup from “B” tubes. Transfer lysates from the previous step to the “B” tubes. Rotate at 4’C for 60 min.
  5. CoIP: Meanwhile, wash antibody-bound beads in 3-3 and 3-4 using 1 ml of PBS-B/T x 3 time (1, 3, and 5 min). When 2nd pre-clearing is complete, transfer the sups to 3-3 and 3-4. Incubate at 4’C for 1.5 hours.
  6. Save sup after coIP (for both Nup153 and neg IgG). Save 20 ul in one tube and rest in another just so that you don’t have to thaw the whole amount later. Store at -20’C.

  • Save Washes 1-7. No need to save the whole amount. 50-100 ul is enough.
  • Elution 1 will contain Nup153 interactors. This can be used for WB or MS.
  • Elution 2 will contain IgG heavy/light chains and antigen protein. In this example, Nup153.
  • Washes and Elutions can be stored at -20’C for a week. Store at -80’C for long-term storage.
